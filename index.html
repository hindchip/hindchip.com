SEMICONDUCTOR DESIGN

Where Ideas Become Silicon

Every semiconductor begins as an idea. Before a chip is manufactured, engineers must design, simulate, verify and optimize billions of transistors to perform specific functions with precision and reliability.




Semiconductor design represents one of the most advanced engineering disciplines in the world. Modern chips integrate extraordinary levels of complexity while balancing performance, power consumption, reliability and manufacturability.


Design activities include system architecture, logic design, verification, physical implementation, testing strategies and optimization across multiple layers of complexity.

As semiconductor systems become increasingly sophisticated, design capability becomes an increasingly important source of innovation and competitive advantage.


Strengthening semiconductor design expertise is therefore a critical component of long-term technology development and ecosystem growth.
